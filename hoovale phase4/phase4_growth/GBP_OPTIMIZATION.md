# 🏢 GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE OPTIMIZATION GUIDE

> **Why this matters more than your website:** For local searches like "wall clock manufacturer near me" or "wall clock supplier Jaipur", Google Business Profile (GBP) results appear ABOVE organic website results. Your GBP is your single biggest local SEO asset.

---

## 🎯 SETUP (One-Time, ~30 Minutes)

### Step 1: Create / Claim Your Profile

1. Visit `business.google.com` → Sign in with business email
2. Search for "HOOVALE" — if existing listing appears, click "Claim"
3. If not, click "Add your business to Google"
4. Enter: business name (HOOVALE), category (Wall Clock Manufacturer / Wholesale Supplier), address (Jaipur factory), phone, website

### Step 2: Verify Your Business

Google will send a verification code via:
- **Postcard** (most common, 5-14 days) — recommended
- **Phone call** (instant, only some businesses)
- **Email** (only some categories)
- **Video** (newer option)

Enter the code in your GBP dashboard. Without verification, your profile won't appear in search results.

## 📸 PHOTOS (Massive Ranking Factor)

GBPs with 100+ photos get 520% more calls than those with no photos. Upload aggressively:

### Mandatory Categories

- [ ] **Logo** (1) — your business logo, 720×720px minimum
- [ ] **Cover photo** (1) — best representative image, 1080×608px
- [ ] **Exterior** (3-5) — factory building, signage, entrance
- [ ] **Interior** (5-10) — production floor, machinery, finished goods storage
- [ ] **Products** (20-30) — best-selling clocks, designer pieces, custom logo examples
- [ ] **Team** (3-5) — staff at work (with permission)
- [ ] **At work** (5-10) — production process, QC, packing
- [ ] **Identity** (1-2) — owner/founder photos (builds trust)

### Photo Best Practices

- Real photos only (Google detects stock images and penalises)
- Good lighting (natural daylight or proper studio lighting)
- High resolution (1920×1080 minimum)
- Update monthly with new photos
- Geotag photos with Jaipur location before uploading (free tool: GeoSetter, ExifTool)

## 💬 REVIEWS (THE BIGGEST RANKING FACTOR)

Reviews directly impact local search ranking. **Aim for 50+ Google reviews with 4.5+ average.**

### Acquisition Strategy

**Week 1-2 — Internal Network:**
- Ask your top 20 customers to leave honest reviews
- Send them a direct review link: `g.page/r/[your-id]/review`
- Provide example review topics: order experience, product quality, delivery, customer service

**Week 3-8 — Active Customers:**
- After every major bulk order delivery, follow up with WhatsApp:
  > "Hi [Name], thank you for your order. If you were satisfied, would you mind sharing a review on Google? It really helps us. Direct link: [link]"

**Ongoing — Review Funnel:**
- Add review request to every dispatch slip
- Email signature: "If we served you well, please leave a Google review"
- After-sale follow-up call (3-5 days post-delivery) ending with review request

### Responding to Reviews

**Every review gets a response within 48 hours.**

For positive reviews:
> "Thank you [Name] for the kind words about our [specific item: bulk order / custom logo printing / fast delivery]. We're glad you had a great experience. Looking forward to serving you again!"

For negative reviews:
> "[Name], thank you for the feedback. We're sorry your experience didn't meet expectations. Please call us at [phone] or email [email] so we can resolve this immediately. We take all feedback seriously."

**Never argue. Never get defensive. Always offer to resolve offline.**

### Avoid Penalties

- Don't buy reviews (Google detects and removes them, can suspend GBP)
- Don't review your own business
- Don't ask employees to leave reviews
- Don't offer incentives for reviews (against Google's terms)

## 🚫 COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Keyword-stuffing business name** ("HOOVALE Wall Clock Manufacturer Jaipur Best Wholesale") — Google penalises, can suspend listing
2. **Using virtual office address** — Google detects and removes; only use real physical location
3. **Multiple GBPs for same business** — Google penalises duplicate listings
4. **Stock photos** — Google detects and ranks lower
5. **Ignoring reviews** — every unaddressed review hurts ranking
6. **No posts for 30+ days** — signals inactive business; ranking drops
7. **Inconsistent NAP** (Name, Address, Phone) across web — must match website, social media, citations exactly
